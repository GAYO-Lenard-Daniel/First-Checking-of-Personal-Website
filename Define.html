So some of you probably aren't aware of this genre...

Well, you've come to the right place! Keep reading to know more and you might be interested on what this genre is. You might even like this more than you think and explore it more.

But maybe some of you may already have an idea as to what folk music is.

You know, something like Mumford & Sons or The Lumineers type of Stomp and Holler from the 2010s featuring anthemic choruses through acoustic instrumentation such as guitar and banjo. Or maybe you’re already familiar with it through viral tiktok songs such as “Ceilings” by Lizzy McAlpine, “Would That I” by Hozier, or “Soldier, Poet, King” by The Oh Hellos, and many others and you just didn’t know that it was or what it was. But you may notice that a consistent sonic thread throughout these songs and artists and that they tend to incorporate an acoustic guitar which may lead you to think that it’s probably just a guy or a bunch of guys with a guitar. And while you’re not too far from the idea, it’s a little too simplistic. So what is Folk music?

However, what folk music was initially, which is in the traditional sense...

is a musical traditions based on particular regions and societies around the world that are orally passed down or through musical notation from generation to generation and are learned by ear. Folk music was played either individually or in groups and were commonly encouraged to join in sessions in public environments using indigenous instruments with common subject matters in folk music such as commemorative, rural, ballads, and political subjects. The term “folk” comes from the German Word “Volk” meaning “the people”. Based on this definition, Folk music is music made to the people, typically associated with regular people or those of the lower class, giving folk music a particularly intimate and open feel for its authentic expression of a way of life with no influence from classical music.
